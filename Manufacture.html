MANUFACTURING

Using machinery and labor to transform the raw materials or components the company has received from its suppliers into something new is the heart of the supply chain management process. This final product is the ultimate goal of the manufacturing process, though it is not the final stage of SCM. The manufacturing process may be further divided into sub-tasks such as assembly, testing, inspection, and packaging. During the manufacturing process, companies must be mindful of waste or other factors that may cause deviations from their original plans. For example, if a company is using more raw materials than planned and sourced for due to inadequate employee training, it must rectify the issue or revisit the earlier stages in SCM.
